four stages of learning
- Latent learning, Cognitive maps, and curiosity (feedback)
"Am I moving in the right direction?" - This question answering with 'RAIL' Framework
> R - Relevance: Stage 1 
(Signs that relevance for you)
1. Lost and Confused
2. Mindblank
3. Overwhelming
(Signs of progression through)
1. Discovery of new variables.
- Active Learning Factors
- Cognitive load contributors
- Different orders of learning
- Encoding and retrieval
2. Irrelevance becomes relevant
(Actions that lead to progression)
1. Exploration
- Get diverse opinions from others
- Learn the theory & other variables
2. Challenging
- Be open minded to other beliefs and assumptions
> A - Awareness: Stage 2
(Lead to progression)
1. Experimentation
- Trying to perform skill correctly and making mistakes
2. Reflection
- Thinking about why you made them
- Consider other variables
- Gather feedback
> I - Iteration: Stage 3
(Signs you are iteration)
1. Able to perform the technique correctly at least once or twice
2. Not discovering new mistakes but struggling to overcome them
(Signs of Progression through)
1. Consistent execution of skill
2. Execution becomes easier
3. Faster (efficiency gains)
(Signs lead to progression)
1. Varied Practice
- Different levels of challenges, context and conditions
- Different subjects, workload, mediums difficulties
2. Adjust
- Observing & Find-tuning
> L - Lifelong: Stage 4
(This stage where to move learning to learn)
