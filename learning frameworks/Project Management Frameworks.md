# Project Management Frameworks

Project management frameworks are foundational sets of processes, tools, and rules used to guide a project from start to finish. They provide structure and standard methodologies to help teams manage resources, timelines, and deliverables.

---

## 1. Agile Frameworks
Designed for flexibility, collaboration, and continuous improvement, allowing teams to adapt to changes quickly.

* **Scrum:** Divides work into short, 1–4 week cycles called "sprints" to encourage iterative development.
* **Kanban:** Uses visual boards to track workflow status and limit work-in-progress to avoid bottlenecks.
* **Extreme Programming (XP):** An agile software development framework focused on teamwork, constant communication, and frequent release cycles.
* **Scrumban:** A hybrid combining Scrum's structured sprint cycles with Kanban's continuous, pull-based workflow.
* **Adaptive Project Framework (APF):** Built on the assumption that project requirements change, adjusting continuously based on stakeholder feedback.

---

## 2. Traditional & Predictive Frameworks
Linear and sequential approaches where every phase of the project is meticulously mapped out from the beginning.

* **Waterfall:** A step-by-step approach where each phase must be completely finished before the next one starts.
* **PRINCE2:** A heavily structured, business-driven method providing template-driven processes favored in European sectors.

---

## 3. Process Improvement & Quality Frameworks
Approaches focused on minimizing waste, cutting costs, and producing highly consistent, defect-free deliverables.

* **Lean:** Focuses on eliminating wasteful practices and maximizing value for the customer.
* **Six Sigma:** Uses statistical, data-driven techniques to minimize variability and eliminate defects using DMAIC or DMADV.

---

## 4. Scheduling & Resource-Focused Frameworks
Ideal for complex projects where timing, dependencies, and resource allocations are the most critical elements.

* **Critical Path Method (CPM):** Maps out the longest sequence of essential dependent tasks required to complete a project on time.
* **Critical Chain Project Management (CCPM):** Builds upon CPM by adding time buffers to account for resource limitations.

---

## 5. Standards & Best Practices
Globally recognized bodies of knowledge and guiding principles that you can apply to any project.

* **PMBOK:** Project Management Body of Knowledge by PMI, acting as the industry-standard guide for PMP certifications.
